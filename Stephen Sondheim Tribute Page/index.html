Stephen Sondheim

Titan of American Musical Theater

Stephen Sondheim, the most innovative, most influential, and most important composer and lyricist in modern Broadway history.

Here is a timeline of Stephen Sondheim's affect on the musical theater world.

  • 1940- Stephen Sondheim began his musical journey by mentoring with Oscar Hammerstein II.
  • 1957- He made his first significant mark on Broadway, though, as the lyricist for Leonard Bernstein’s West Side Story.
  • 1959- He then wrote the lyrics for Gypsy (music by Jule Styne)
  • 1960- Stephen Sondheim lost his mentor and father figure when Hammerstein died of stomach cancer on August 23, aged 65.
  • 1962- A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum—based on comedies by the Roman playwright Plautus—opened on Broadway, with music and lyrics by Sondheim. It ran for 964 performances and won the Tony Award for best musical.
  • 1964- Ater three straight hits, his next musical, Anyone Can Whistle closed after only nine performances
  • 1965- Sondheim contributed the lyrics for Richard Rodger's Do I Hear a Waltz? solidifying his decision to only work if he could write both the lyrics and music.
  • 1970-1979- He won Tony Awards for best score for Company (1970), Follies (1971), A Little Night Music (1973; film 1977), and Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street (1979; film 2007)
  • 1981- Sondheim founded Young Playwrights Inc. to introduce young people to writing for the theater, and was the organization's executive vice-president.
  • 1984- Sondheim next collaborated with playwright-director James Lapine to create Sunday in the Park with George.
  • 1987- They collaborated again to bring forth Into the Woods, which again brought in a Tony Award for best score.
  • 1994- Passion, with a run of 280 performances, was the shortest-running show to win a Tony Award for Best Musical.
  • 2000- He received the Japan Art Association’s Praemium Imperiale prize for theatre/film
  • 2008- He was honoured with a special Tony Award for lifetime achievement in the theatre
  • 2015- Sondheim was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama in a ceremony at the White House.
  • 2021- Sondheim died of cardiovascular disease at his home in Roxbury, on November 26, 2021, at the age of 91.

"I love the theater as much as music, and the whole idea of getting across to an audience and making them laugh, making them cry - just making them feel - is paramount to me."

-Stephen Sondheim
